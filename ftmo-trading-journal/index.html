FTMO Rules Are Risk Rules.
R-value enforces them automatically.

FTMO's trading objectives — 10% profit target, 5% daily loss limit, 10% maximum drawdown — are all percentage-of-equity constraints (our FTMO challenge rules explained guide breaks each one down). R-Multiple thinking enforces the same constraints as your position sizing methodology. When 1R is defined as 1% of your current funded equity, your daily loss limit becomes a simple R count. No dollar-amount monitoring alongside your sizing decisions.

After a losing sequence, your funded equity decreases. Because SignalDeck syncs your live FTMO balance, your 1R dollar amount decreases with it — you're automatically de-sizing after drawdown without changing any settings. This is what live balance sync makes possible.

Know If Your Edge Is Real
Before You Pay the Challenge Fee.

An FTMO Challenge costs $155–$1,080+ depending on account size. Most traders pay to find out whether their strategy works under prop firm conditions. SignalDeck lets you find that out first.

Walk-Forward Analysis splits your backtest data into in-sample and out-of-sample windows, returns a Pass or Likely Overfitted verdict, and shows the walk-forward efficiency ratio. A passing strategy has demonstrated that its parameters hold up on data they were never fit to — which is the closest thing to a live test you can run before you're live.

Monte Carlo simulation reruns your trade sequence 1,000 times in randomized order. The 5th percentile result is your worst-case drawdown under bad luck conditions — the drawdown your historical sequence happened to avoid.
