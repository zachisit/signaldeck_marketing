R-Value in a Volatile Market.
The framework matters more, not less.

Crypto's volatility doesn't change the math — it amplifies the consequences of ignoring it. An oversized BTC position can turn a 5% adverse move into a catastrophic loss. R-Multiple tracking forces you to define risk before every entry and hold it there.

SQN tells you whether your system has a statistically real edge or whether you've been lucky in a bull market. Kelly Criterion sizes your positions from your own win rate and average R — not a generic 1% rule someone posted on Twitter.

Spot & Leverage.
Same framework, both modes.

Whether you're buying spot BTC or trading a leveraged perpetual, SignalDeck logs the position in R-terms. Leverage multiplies P&L but it doesn't change the risk framework — if you defined your risk as 1R before entry, that number is what matters regardless of notional size.

Log fractional coin amounts. Tag by exchange. Log your leverage factor alongside the entry. The Post Mortem will ask the same questions it asks for any trade: was the setup sound, was the stop held, was the management disciplined?

Post Mortems for Crypto.
Process, not just outcome.

Crypto markets move fast. FOMO is louder. The urge to size up after a big win — or revenge trade after a liquidation — is constant. The Post Mortem protocol is designed specifically to short-circuit those patterns.

After every closed trade: Setup Diagnosis, Management Diagnosis, Final Verdict, Training Gap, Mentor Feedback. When you run 50 Post Mortems and see "FOMO entry" or "Didn't wait for confirmation" appearing in 60% of your bad losses — that changes your behavior permanently.
