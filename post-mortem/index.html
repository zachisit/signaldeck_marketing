Why Most Trade Reviews Are Useless

The typical trade review goes like this: look at the chart, see where you entered and exited, feel bad if it's red, feel good if it's green, move on. This teaches you nothing because it confuses outcome with process.

A losing trade can be the result of a correct, disciplined process — the setup was valid, the management was sound, the loss was pure variance. A winning trade can be the product of a broken process — you sized too large, held through a violation of your rules, and got bailed out by luck. Reviewing only the P&L treats both as if they're the same thing.

The Post Mortem protocol separates the outcome from the process. That's the only review worth doing.

The 5-Step Post Mortem Protocol

Required for every closed trade in SignalDeck. Not a free-text notes field — a structured diagnostic sequence.

01

Setup Diagnosis

Was the setup valid according to your rules at the time of entry? This is judged at the moment you entered — not with hindsight. A valid setup that lost is different from an invalid setup that won. You are diagnosing the entry decision, not the outcome.

02

Management Diagnosis

Once the trade was on, did you manage it according to plan? Did you move your stop early? Exit before your target? Add to a losing position? Management errors are separate from setup errors — and often more expensive over time because they override the edge you spent months building.

03

Verdict

Was this a good process trade or a bad process trade? This verdict is independent of P&L. A good process loss is a signal to keep trading the system. A bad process win is a warning to examine whether you got lucky. The verdict drives the learning, not the dollar sign.

04

Training Gap

What specific skill, habit, or knowledge needs work? Not "be more disciplined" — that's too vague. "I need to stop entering before the 8:30 data release" or "I need to pre-mark my stop before entering, not after." Specific, actionable, one thing at a time.

05

Mentor Feedback

What would a more experienced, emotionally detached version of you say about this trade? This step forces you to step outside the emotional reaction to the P&L and evaluate your decisions from a third-person perspective. It's the closest thing to having a trading coach in the room with you.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you review a losing trade?

Go through the 5 steps: Setup Diagnosis (was the entry valid?), Management Diagnosis (did you manage it to plan?), Verdict (good process or bad process?), Training Gap (what specific improvement is needed?), Mentor Feedback (what would a rational, experienced trader say?). The goal is to separate the loss from your process quality — they are not always the same thing.

Should you review winning trades too?

Yes. A winning trade on bad process teaches the wrong lessons and often leads to repeating the mistake at larger size. A losing trade on good process confirms your rules work and the loss was variance. The Verdict step makes this distinction explicit on every trade.

How long should a trade post mortem take?

3–5 minutes per trade is enough if you're using a structured protocol. Free-text journaling takes longer and produces less consistent data.
