Frequently Asked Questions

Do trading journals show news on the symbols you trade?

Almost none do — most journals are post-trade analytics with no news layer, so you keep a separate news tab or terminal open. SignalDeck pulls market news for the exact symbol on each trade, inside the journal, and archives the headlines from around your entry so you can review what the tape was reacting to.

What is news at trade entry?

When you review a closed trade, SignalDeck shows the headlines published in the window around your entry timestamp — 24 hours either side by default, adjustable up to 7 days. It answers "what was the market reacting to when I got in?" from your own archived news, so your post-mortem includes the context, not just the price action.

Which asset classes get news coverage?

Live today: stocks and equity ETFs get symbol-specific headlines; CFD indices map to a liquid proxy (US500 → SPY, US100 → QQQ) for index-level news; metals like XAUUSD map to GLD; and crypto gets per-coin news. Forex and futures coverage is coming soon. Where no symbol-specific source exists, you get honest market/macro headlines — never the wrong symbol's news.

Are the headlines sentiment-tagged?

Yes. Each headline is tagged positive, neutral or negative where the source provides sentiment, so you can scan the tone of the tape on a symbol at a glance rather than reading every article.

Which plan includes Market News?

Market News is an Elite plan feature ($50/mo when billing launches ~June 2026), and it's free for everyone during the beta. The live news feed, the per-trade News tab, the dedicated News screen, and news-at-entry are all included on Elite.
